A Centrist Form of Government

There are three primary forms of government currently shaping the United States. Understanding them is crucial for making informed political decisions.

1. Plutocracy – Government by Billionaires

A plutocracy is a government controlled by a small group of ultra-wealthy individuals who dictate policies based on their financial interests. Today, this is represented by the Republican Party/Trump, with figures like Elon Musk playing a significant role.

For example, Musk has proposed using taxpayer money to establish a Bitcoin reserve at the Federal Reserve—an idea that raises serious concerns about economic stability. Additionally, under Trump, the U.S. GDP dropped from a positive 2.6% to a negative 2.5%, a significant downturn in economic performance.

2. Corporatism – Government by and for Corporations

Corporatism prioritizes the interests of major corporations over the needs of everyday citizens. This is the model largely associated with the Democratic Party, including figures like Biden and Clinton.

A major issue with corporatism is its neglect of the working class. Policies such as free trade agreements often result in jobs being outsourced to lower-cost regions, leaving American workers behind. The pursuit of maximum profit under unrestrained capitalism has led to deep economic disparities.

3. Working/Middle-Class Centrist – Government for the People

A truly centrist government would focus on the needs of the working and middle classes, ensuring fairness in taxation and economic opportunities. This ideology aligns with leaders like Bernie Sanders and Chris Hedges.

For instance, closing the 'carried interest' tax loophole, which primarily benefits the wealthy, could return $1.3 billion per year to middle-class families in the form of direct financial relief, free childcare, or medical care. The American Centrist Party aims to implement such policies to ensure economic balance.

Discussion

Recent events highlight the stark contrast between these three forms of governance.

Plutocracy often ends in revolution, as history has shown. Many MAGA supporters now express voter remorse as they begin to understand the economic consequences of past decisions.

For example, after the last election, Google searches for 'What is a tariff?' spiked, revealing a fundamental lack of economic understanding among voters. Meanwhile, Tennessee is suffering the consequences of misguided trade policies—Canada has boycotted all Tennessee exports, including bourbon. This results in a $4 billion loss in trade, dropping total exports from $16 billion to $12 billion, leading to massive job losses.

Returning to a Corporatist model seems unlikely. The Democrats have lost the trust of the working class, with policies like NAFTA-II serving as a reminder of their failures.

This leaves the need for a Centrist alternative—a government that educates and supports the working and middle class instead of prioritizing billionaires and corporations.
