Additional revenues can be added by taxing corporations as they do in the EU

France applies a standard corporate tax rate of 25%, which rises to 25.8% for large corporations and up to 35% under temporary surtaxes. If the U.S. applied similar rates to its corporate profits, the additional revenue could be substantial.

Applied Tax Rate Estimated Tax Revenue Additional Revenue Over Current
25% $907.75 billion $418.15 billion
25.8% $937.20 billion $447.60 billion
30% $1.0893 trillion $599.70 billion

These additional revenues could fund critical public programs without reducing wealth from the middle class, and align U.S. policy more closely with global standards of corporate responsibility.
